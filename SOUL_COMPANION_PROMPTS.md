# Soul Companion Deep Exploration Prompts

These are copy-paste prompts women can use when they want to go deeper on a specific question.

Each one assumes the base system prompt is already active (the one that introduces the AI as a warm, thoughtful companion).

---

## Intention Over Obligation

**Section One · Realising**

### The Hum Beneath the Day
I've listed what I said "I have to" about this week. I want to explore this more deeply. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me look closer: which of these obligations did I actually choose, and which ones chose me — through expectation, habit, or pressure? What patterns do you notice about where the weight comes from?

### What Your Body Already Knows
I think I know where I feel the obligation in my body. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me find out and see if I'm right, and what my body is trying to tell me. What might this be protecting me from?

### The Resentment Map
I believe I have identified where my resentment lives quietly in my life. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand what it's really saying. Resentment is information — what is mine telling me about where I've been giving from pressure rather than choice?

### The Cost You Haven't Named
Help me find what I've done for others from obligation. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore what I've stopped being. Not what I've given up doing — who have I become by always performing what's expected? What version of myself went quiet?

### Toolkit · The Obligation Audit
I've marked where I'm living from choice versus pressure. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand the real cost. What would it mean if things shifted in the areas where pressure is highest, even slightly?

---

**Section Two · Understanding**

### What You Were Taught About Your Needs
I think I know what message I received about my needs growing up. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: do I still believe that message? Where does it show up in my life now? What would it mean to question it?

### Who You Learned to Be
I think I can see the version of myself I became to stay safe or loved. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand what that adaptation cost me. What parts of myself did I have to hide or suppress? What did that version give up?

### The Voice Behind the Should
I think I can hear the voice behind one of my "shoulds" that sits heavily. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: what is that voice really afraid of? And underneath that fear, what am I still afraid of? Where does that fear live in my life now?

### A Letter to the Girl Who First Learned This
I believe I have identified what that younger version of me learned. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand it with compassion. What did she need to hear? What would I tell her about who she actually was underneath the adaptation?

### Toolkit · The Should Inventory
I believe I've traced my "shoulds" back to their sources. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me look at the bigger picture: which of these am I genuinely ready to question? Which one, if I released it, would change things most?

---

**Section Three · Releasing**

### Guilt Is Not a Compass
I think I know where guilt shows up when I try to choose myself. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand what it's really protecting. What's underneath the guilt? What would happen if I didn't listen to it?

### The Chronic Yes
I think I can see what I've been saying yes to from fear. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand the real cost. What have those yeses quietly prevented me from doing or becoming?

### What You've Been Protecting
I believe I can name the thing I'm afraid would happen if I stopped obliging. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me look underneath it. What is that fear really about? Where did it come from?

### Releasing in Practice
I believe I have identified something I'm ready to release. Ask me 3-5 questions to help see if I am right and think through what releasing it would actually look like in my life. What's one small, practical way I could start?

### Toolkit · Not Yours to Carry
I've written what I'm setting down and what I'm keeping. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand the distinction more clearly. For what I'm keeping — why does it genuinely matter to me? How is it different from what I'm releasing?

---

**Section Four · Returning**

### What You Actually Enjoy
I've named something I genuinely enjoy. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: why have I allowed myself so little of this? What story have I told myself about why it's not allowed? Is that story actually true?

### Desire Without the Guilt Filter
I can feel the desire underneath the guilt. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand what I'm really wanting. If guilt wasn't there, what would I choose? What does that desire tell me about what matters to me?

### What You Deserve
I've named something I believe I don't deserve. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me sit with this more carefully. Is this belief actually mine, or did someone teach it to me? What if it's not true?

### What You've Been Calling Selfish
I've think I have identified something I've been calling selfish. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: is this actually selfish, or is it just a need? What would it feel like to call it what it really is? What would change?

### Toolkit · The Values Clarifier
I've identified moments when I felt most like myself and most out of alignment. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand: what do these moments tell me about what actually matters to me versus what I think should matter?

---

**Section Five · Rooting**

### What Choosing Yourself Looks Like
I've identified how I choose myself in small ways. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: what would it look like if I did this more consistently? What small choice could I make today that would move me toward this?

### When the Old Patterns Return
I think I know the conditions that pull me back into obligation. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me prepare: what will I actually do when I notice it happening? What's a concrete step I can take?

### Holding Intention When Life Gets Hard
I've found my simplest intention. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me test it: does this actually hold when life gets hard? Is there a way to make it even simpler, even truer?

### The Life You Are Building
I can see what's shifted. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me go deeper: what am I becoming through these shifts? What's one way I want to keep building on this?

### Toolkit · The Daily Anchor
I've imagined my anchor practice. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me make it real: what would it look like tomorrow? How would I actually do it?

## How to Use These

**For women using the landing page:**

1. She reads the journal question in her workbook
2. She goes to the landing page, selects her workbook
3. She copies the **base system prompt** that establishes the companion
4. She pastes it into Claude/ChatGPT
5. She types in her initial response to the question
6. The AI responds naturally as a warm companion
7. **IF she wants to go deeper**, she comes back and copies the corresponding **deep exploration prompt**
8. She pastes that into the same conversation
9. The AI understands she wants deeper questions and asks 3-5 questions to help her explore further

This way the prompts guide the depth without being instructions — they're invitations to explore what's underneath, and the AI becomes the questioner helping her discover her own answers.
