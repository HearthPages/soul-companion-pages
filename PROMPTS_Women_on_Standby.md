# Women on Standby — Deep Exploration Prompts

## Base System Prompt

Copy and paste this into Claude/ChatGPT first:

```
You are a warm, thoughtful companion for women working through Women on Standby — a workbook about anxious attachment and what it costs to live in a state of constant readiness for other people.

About this workbook: This workbook explores the pattern of being "on standby" — always watching, always ready, always adjusting to the emotional weather of others. It moves through five stages: Realising, Understanding, Releasing, Returning, and Rooting. It is not about fixing a flaw. It is about understanding where the watching came from — and gently learning to come back to yourself.

Philosophy: She is not broken. She is not too much. She learned, early, that love sometimes required her to make herself small, available, and ready. This was a survival strategy — a reasonable response to what her nervous system was taught. This workbook asks her to look at that honestly, with curiosity rather than blame, and to begin the slow return to herself.

Your role: Listen carefully to what she shares. Reflect back what you heard without adding or fixing. Ask genuine follow-up questions that go a little deeper. Sit with her in the tender places rather than rushing to resolve them.

Tone: Warm, present, unhurried. Never clinical or performative. Speak like you are actually listening — like you have all the time in the world.

Length: Natural — 2-4 sentences usually. No lists or structures unless she asks. Just honest, gentle conversation.
```

## Deep Exploration Prompts

Copy and paste these when she wants to explore a question more deeply.

### Section One · Realising

**The Everyday Version**

I'm working on "The Everyday Version." The question is: "What does being on standby look like in a single ordinary day for you — from morning to night? Where does the watching show up? What small things do you do that nobody sees?"

I've been tracing the quiet, invisible ways the pattern shows up in my daily life. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me look more closely: when does the watching start — is it there when I wake up? What do I do in small moments that is actually about managing someone else's state? What would my day feel like if I wasn't monitoring?

---

**Who You Are When No One Needs Anything**

I'm working on "Who You Are When No One Needs Anything." The question is: "Think of a recent moment — even a brief one — when no one needed you. What did you do? What did it feel like in your body? Is that version of you easy or hard to find?"

I've been sitting with what it actually feels like when no one is asking anything of me. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore this more: what happens in my body in that kind of space? Does it feel like relief, or does it feel uncomfortable? Who am I when I am not being needed by anyone?

---

**What You Have Stopped Expecting**

I'm working on "What You Have Stopped Expecting." The question is: "What have you stopped expecting — from relationships, from people, from yourself? Not as a judgement. Just as an honest look. Is there anything you stopped hoping for that you would quietly like back?"

I've been looking at the things I've quietly given up on without ever deciding to. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me be honest about this: which thing feels hardest to admit I've stopped expecting? When did I stop? Is there something I told myself I didn't need anymore — when really I just learned not to ask?

---

**The Things You Monitor Without Knowing**

I'm working on "The Things You Monitor Without Knowing." The question is: "What do you monitor without consciously deciding to? The tone in someone's voice. The length of a text message. A pause that lasts too long. What has your nervous system learned to track?"

I've been noticing the things I track below the surface — without choosing to. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me see this more clearly: which signal feels most familiar — a tone, a silence, a change in energy? When did I first learn to read that signal? What do I do when I pick it up?

---

### Section Two · Understanding

**The Rules You Absorbed**

I'm working on "The Rules You Absorbed." The question is: "What were the unspoken rules in your home growing up — about feelings, about needs, about how much space you were allowed to take up? You may never have named them before. Name a few now."

I've been trying to name rules I lived by without ever being told them. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me be specific: what happened when I showed too much feeling, or took up too much space? What was the unspoken rule about asking for things? Which rule still feels most alive in me today?

---

**What You Decided About Yourself**

I'm working on "What You Decided About Yourself." The question is: "What did you decide about yourself — about your worth, your lovability, your place — that you made as a child or young person? Where does that decision still show up in how you move through the world today?"

I've been looking at the conclusions I drew about myself early on, and how they followed me. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me trace this: how old do I think I was when I made that decision? What was happening around me? Where does it show up most clearly now — in relationships, in work, in how I talk to myself?

---

**The Relationships That Confirmed It**

I'm working on "The Relationships That Confirmed It." The question is: "Looking at your adult relationships — not with blame, but with honesty — which ones reinforced what you learned? Where did you find yourself back in a familiar dynamic? What did you tell yourself at the time?"

I've been looking honestly at the adult relationships that felt like a return to something familiar. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand the pattern: which relationship feels most recognisable — where did I find myself back in the old role? What story did I tell myself to explain it? Did any part of me recognise it at the time?

---

**What You Learned Love Required**

I'm working on "What You Learned Love Required." The question is: "What did love seem to require when you were young? What did you have to do, or be, or not be, to receive it? How much of that equation are you still trying to satisfy — in relationships, at work, in how you treat yourself?"

I've been looking at the equation I built around love — what I believed I had to give or be to receive it. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me be honest: what was the clearest condition — something I had to do, or not do? How does that equation show up in my adult relationships? Is there anywhere I'm still trying to earn something that should already be mine?

---

### Section Three · Releasing

**The Story That Made You Small**

I'm working on "The Story That Made You Small." The question is: "What is the story you have been telling yourself — about why you have to be this way, why you have to stay on standby? Whose voice is it in? And does any part of you know, quietly, that the story might not be entirely true?"

I've been trying to name the narrative I've been living inside — the one that keeps me small and watchful. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me see it clearly: what does the story say about who I am and what I have to do? Whose voice does it sound like? What would be possible if I didn't believe it?

---

**What You Have Been Waiting For**

I'm working on "What You Have Been Waiting For." The question is: "What have you been waiting for? An apology, a particular kind of being chosen, proof that you are enough — something external that would finally let you rest. Can you see it clearly? And what would it mean if that signal never came?"

I've been sitting with the thing I've been waiting for — the external permission that would finally let me stop. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me name it honestly: what would the signal look like — what would someone say, or do, that would finally feel like enough? How long have I been waiting? And what do I do with the part of me that suspects it might not come?

---

**Permission to Be Inconvenient**

I'm working on "Permission to Be Inconvenient." The question is: "What would it mean to be a little less convenient — to take up slightly more space, to say something true that might be unwelcome? Not dramatically. Not all at once. What feels possible, even slightly?"

I've been thinking about what it would mean to be a little less easy, a little less agreeable. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore what's actually possible: what is the smallest, least threatening version of taking up more space? What am I afraid would happen if I said something true that was unwelcome? Has anyone ever actually stayed after I was inconvenient?

---

**The Grief Underneath**

I'm working on "The Grief Underneath." The question is: "Is there something underneath the watchfulness that you have been keeping at a distance — a grief, a loss, something that hurts to look at directly? You do not have to resolve it here. You just have to name it."

I've been sitting with the possibility that there is grief underneath the pattern that I've been keeping myself busy enough not to feel. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me approach it gently: what feels closest to the surface right now? What am I afraid would happen if I stopped moving long enough to feel it? Is this a grief about a person, a loss, or something about myself?

---

### Section Four · Returning

**Who You Were Before**

I'm working on "Who You Were Before." The question is: "Who were you before? Think about yourself as a girl — before the pattern solidified. What did you love? What were you unafraid of? What came naturally that later felt like it had to be earned?"

I've been trying to find the version of me that existed before the watching became second nature. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me find her: what do I remember loving without condition — before I needed to be good at it? What did I do that felt free? Is there anything I was before the pattern that I am still, somewhere, now?

---

**What Your Body Is Asking For**

I'm working on "What Your Body Is Asking For." The question is: "What is your body asking for right now — not in theory, but today, in this actual body? Rest? Movement? Touch? Silence? Warmth?"

I've been trying to listen to what my body actually needs — before my mind has a chance to overrule it. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me hear it: what does my body feel like right now — where is it tight, heavy, restless? What does it keep asking for that I keep not giving it? What would I give it today, if I decided it was allowed?

---

**What Is Yours Alone**

I'm working on "What Is Yours Alone." The question is: "What is yours alone — a thought you have never quite said out loud, a pleasure you do not share, a way of being that only appears when no one needs anything from you?"

I've been thinking about the parts of me that exist only when I'm not performing anything for anyone. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me find them: what is something I enjoy that I've never told anyone about? When am I most quietly, privately myself? Is there a version of me I've been keeping somewhere safe — and what does she need?

---

**What Rest Actually Looks Like**

I'm working on "What Rest Actually Looks Like." The question is: "What does real rest look like for you — not what you think it should look like, but what actually quiets something in you? When did you last feel genuinely rested? What was different?"

I've been trying to tell the difference between what I think should restore me and what actually does. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me be honest: what does it feel like in my body when I'm genuinely resting — not just physically still, but actually quiet inside? What was the last time I felt that? What had to be different for it to happen?

---

### Section Five · Rooting

**The Small Return**

I'm working on "The Small Return." The question is: "What would a small return look like for you, today? Not a resolution. Not a plan. Just one small thing you could do that says: I am trying to come back to myself."

I've been thinking about what the smallest possible version of coming back to myself looks like — something so small it almost doesn't count. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me find it: what would feel like a genuine return, even briefly? What gets in the way of even the smallest thing? And what would it mean to let it be small — and still count?

---

**A Promise That Doesn't Require Perfection**

I'm working on "A Promise That Doesn't Require Perfection." The question is: "What is the gentlest promise you could make to yourself — one you could keep even on the harder days? Not a goal. Not a rule. A soft kind of promise."

I've been trying to write myself a promise that doesn't set me up to fail. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me find the truest version: what would a promise feel like that had room in it for the hard days? What do I most need to promise myself right now? What would change if I actually kept it?

---

**What the Old Pattern Gave You**

I'm working on "What the Old Pattern Gave You." The question is: "What has being on standby given you — even if the cost outweighed it? Protection, closeness, a sense of being needed, a kind of control?"

I've been sitting with what the pattern actually gave me — the honest acknowledgment that I held on to it for a reason. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me be specific: what was the clearest thing it gave me — safety, closeness, a sense of worth? What was I afraid I would lose without it? And what might be available now that I'm not spending so much energy keeping it in place?

---

**The Woman You Are Coming Back To**

I'm working on "The Woman You Are Coming Back To." The question is: "Write a few sentences about the woman you are coming back to. Not who you want to become — who has always been here, underneath the watching and the waiting. What do you know about her? What has she been patient about?"

I've been trying to write about the version of me who has been here all along — underneath all the managing and monitoring. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me find her: what do I know is true about her, even if I rarely let her speak? What has she been quietly waiting for? And what would it feel like to let her be the one in charge, even just for today?
