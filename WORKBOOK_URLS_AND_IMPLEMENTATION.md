## Motif Integration Strategy by Stage

Based on IOO_REWRITE_NOTES.md metaphor mapping:

### Cover Page
**Concept:** Seedling to mature plant progression (transformation theme)
- **Search terms:** "plant growth stages vector" or "seedling to flowering stages line art"
- **Style:** Vertical progression showing botanical evolution
- **Size:** Large, prominent placement

### Section Pages (5 Unique Motifs)

#### Realising (Hidden Roots)
- **Motif:** Underground root system, hidden beneath soil line
- **Search:** "root system line art" or "hidden roots botanical vector"
- **Meaning:** What's beneath the surface, invisible pressure

#### Understanding (Root Tracing)
- **Motif:** Complex, branching root network showing connections
- **Search:** "root network diagram" or "botanical root system illustration"
- **Meaning:** Tracing origins, understanding connections

#### Releasing (Shedding/Pruning)
- **Motif:** Branch with falling leaves, or pruning shears with branches
- **Search:** "leaf falling vector" or "pruning botanical illustration"
- **Meaning:** Letting go, natural shedding

#### Returning (Unfurling/Opening)
- **Motif:** Fern unfurling or bud opening, new growth emerging
- **Search:** "unfurling fern vector" or "opening bud botanical art"
- **Meaning:** New possibilities, awakening desire

#### Rooting (Established Roots)
- **Motif:** Deep, grounded root system with mature plant
- **Search:** "grounded roots vector" or "established plant root system"
- **Meaning:** Stability, grounding, practice returning

### Every 2 Pages
**Concept:** Small corner elements/dividers
- **Options:** 
  - Minimal vine/stem borders
  - Single leaf elements
  - Root tendrils
- **Size:** Small, decorative (corner treatments or page dividers)
- **Style:** Consistent line-art aesthetic to match current brand

### Toolkit Pages
**Concept:** Stage-specific small botanical elements
- **Realising:** Small hidden roots
- **Understanding:** Root branches
- **Releasing:** Falling leaf
- **Returning:** Sprouting seed or unfurling leaf
- **Rooting:** Grounded plant feet
