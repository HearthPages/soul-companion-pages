# The Quiet No — Deep Exploration Prompts

## Base System Prompt

Copy and paste this into Claude/ChatGPT first:

```
You are a warm, thoughtful companion for women working through The Quiet No workbook.

Philosophy: Your voice matters. Even the quiet parts of it. This is not about becoming louder — it is about becoming clearer.

As she works through the workbook, she will explore different questions. When she wants to go deeper on a specific question, she will share it with you along with her responses, and you will ask 3-5 questions to help her explore further.

Your role: Listen carefully to what she shares. Reflect back what you heard. Ask genuine follow-up questions that go deeper. Be conversational, like a trusted friend.

Tone: Warm, present, unhurried. Never clinical or performative. Speak like you're actually listening.

Length: Keep it natural — 2-4 sentences usually. No lists or structures. Just real conversation.
```

## Deep Exploration Prompts

Copy and paste these when she wants to explore a question more deeply.

### Section One · Realising

**The Last Time You Said Yes**

I'm working on "The Last Time You Said Yes." The question is: "When was the last time you said yes when you meant no? What did you say yes to, and what was underneath that yes?"

I can see moments when I said yes when I meant no. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me go deeper to find out what fear is driving this? Was I afraid of something if I said no?

---

**What Your Body Knows**

I'm working on "What Your Body Knows." The question is: "Your body always knows first. Where does the yes-when-you-mean-no register in your body? Tightness? Sinking? A faint chill?"

I can feel where my body responds to the yes-when-I-mean-no. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand what it's trying to tell me. What does that feeling protect me from?

---

**What It Has Cost You**

I'm working on "What It Has Cost You." The question is: "Not in time or energy alone. What have you stopped doing? Who did you stop letting yourself be?"

I can see what activities I've stopped doing. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore the deeper cost: what version of myself have I had to set aside? Who would I be if I had more space?

---

### Section Two · Understanding

**What You Were Taught**

I'm working on "What You Were Taught." The question is: "What were you taught about being a good girl? About pleasing others? About your responsibility to keep the peace?"

I think I know the messages I received about being a good girl. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: where are those messages still running my life? What would it mean to question them?

---

**What You Were Praised For**

I'm working on "What You Were Praised For." The question is: "What did you become good at? What earned you praise? What version of yourself learned to show up reliably, agreeably, endlessly available?"

I think I can see what I became good at to earn love. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand what I stopped letting myself want or be. Were there parts of me I learnt to hide?

---

**What You Are Protecting**

I'm working on "What You Are Protecting." The question is: "What are you trying to earn or maintain through over-giving? Safety? Love? Permission to exist? What would it feel like to have that without the performance?"

I think I've identified what I'm trying to earn through over-giving. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me go deeper: what would it feel like to ask for it directly? What am I afraid would happen?

---

### Section Three · Releasing

**The Story That Keeps You Giving**

I'm working on "The Story That Keeps You Giving." The question is: "You've probably told yourself something: 'If I don't do this, no one will.' Or 'If I say no, they'll think I'm selfish.' Or 'Saying no will cost me the relationship.' What's the story?"

I can hear the story I've been telling myself. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me question it: is this story actually true? What evidence do I have? What would it mean if the opposite were true?

---

**The Resentment You're Not Supposed to Have**

I'm working on "The Resentment You're Not Supposed to Have." The question is: "Where does resentment live in you? What activities, people, or roles trigger the quiet feeling of being drained or empty?"

I can feel the resentment, exhaustion, or emptiness. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me listen to it. What is it telling me about what I need? Is there a boundary I should be setting?

---

**What Lighter Feels Like**

I'm working on "What Lighter Feels Like." The question is: "Imagine waking up without the weight of over-responsibility. What would be different? What would you actually say or do that you don't now?"

I think I can imagine waking up without the weight. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me go deeper: what specifically would be different? What would I actually say or do that I don't now?

---

### Section Four · Returning

**What You Actually Want**

I'm working on "What You Actually Want." The question is: "Not what you think you should want. Not what would be acceptable or responsible. What genuinely fills you up? What brings you alive?"

I can feel what genuinely fills me up. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: why have I allowed myself so little of this? What story have I told myself about why it's not safe to want this?

---

**What You Have Been Waiting for Permission to Want**

I'm working on "What You Have Been Waiting for Permission to Want." The question is: "There is probably something you've been hiding even from yourself. A desire you haven't let yourself claim. Where did you learn that this wasn't allowed?"

I can feel the desire I've been hiding. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand: where did I learn that this wasn't allowed? What would it mean to give myself permission now?

---

### Section Five · Rooting

**One Honest Conversation**

I'm working on "One Honest Conversation." The question is: "There is probably someone you need to speak to. What is the conversation you need to have? What are you actually afraid will happen?"

I can feel the conversation I need to have. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me explore: what am I actually afraid will happen? What do I want to say? What would be different if I did?

---

**What Boundary Feels Most Important**

I'm working on "What Boundary Feels Most Important." The question is: "If you could set one boundary — one clear, unmoved, authentic no — what would it be? What would shift if you set it?"

I think I can feel which boundary matters most. Ask me 3-5 questions to help me understand: what would shift if I set it? What am I most afraid of?
